## Story

A group of wandering adventurers, after a long and taxing walk, stumble across a town to stay the night in. Once they find an Inn, they go to bed in hopes of waking up in the morning refreshed and ready to continue their travels. Unfortunately, they’re woken in the middle of a night by a ruckus going on outside. Turns out that the town they’ve decided to stay in is under siege.

The adventurers discover that the invaders come from the **Zelshiky Tribe**. They valiantly try to defend the town but are too inexperienced and weak to hold them off for very long, eventually falling back with the rest of the town into the nearby cave. After the tribe finally leaves, the adventurers exit the cave to find the town destroyed.

The adventurers decide to help **Cidel**, a priest and the leader of the town, reconstruct his town, assigned the task of getting supplies from **King Valdos**, who resides in the neighboring town. Still, the Zelshiky Tribe remains at bay, a fearful enemy. The heroes know they must face this foe to avenge the fallen innocents. What they discover though is a dark and twisted history of greed that sheds light on the true savage and the mystery of the powerful **Harmony Gems**.

## Heroes

### Toxophilite

  * **Unit:** Ghost
  * **Health Range:** 891–2700
  * **Damage:** 80
  * **Strengths:**
      * Most range of any other hero.
      * Deals the most damage at start of game.
      * Weapons can be upgraded to 30 as opposed to 25.
  * **Weaknesses:**
      * Concussive damage. Deals less damage to bigger units.
      * Second weakest hero in terms of health.
  * **Abilities:**
      * **Many Shot:** Creates ghosts around you for a second who unleash a flurry of shots all at once.
      * **Allure:** Attracts all enemies to a particular spot, distracting them and allowing you to shoot at will.
      * **Ricochet:** Creates mutalisks around you for a while who shoot powerful spikes that bounce, hitting multiple enemies at a time.
      * **Arrow Fury:** Spawns observers that act as arrows. They gather around the hero and then shoot out in all directions, exploding and killing anything in its blast instantly.

### Thief

  * **Unit:** Dark Templar
  * **Health Range:** 1089–3300
  * **Damage:** 40
  * **Strengths:**
      * Fastest moving hero.
      * Earns 40% more money per kill.
  * **Weaknesses:**
      * Slow melee attack.
  * **Abilities:**
      * **Traps:** Creates mines around the hero that deal tons of damage to enemies.
      * **Invisibility:** Turns the hero invisible, allowing him to roam around without being attacked.
      * **Accomplices:** Creates dark templar around the hero for a while that aid him in hand-to-hand combat.
      * **Bribe:** Turns most enemies around the hero into computer allies that will fight off any foes remaining.

### Barbarian

  * **Unit:** Zealot
  * **Health Range:** 1485–4500
  * **Damage:** 60
  * **Strengths:**
      * Armor can be upgraded to 25 as opposed to 20.
      * Has the highest amount of health throughout the game.
      * Deals a healthy amount of damage (2nd highest).
  * **Weaknesses:**
      * Slow movement speed.
      * Needs 25% more experience each level up.
  * **Abilities:**
      * **Upheaval:** Creates lurkers underneath the hero for a few seconds to help kill large groups of enemies.
      * **Trample:** The hero can ram into his enemies to instantly kill them; useful for escaping or taking out tough targets.
      * **Brothers in Arms:** Creates an ultralisk that will stand side by side the hero in heavy combat.
      * **Earthquake:** All units within the vicinity shake uncontrollably (immobile), allowing the hero to pick them off.

### Mage

  * **Unit:** High Templar
  * **Health Range:** 792–2400
  * **Damage:** 55
  * **Strengths:**
      * Max mana of 1500 as opposed to 1000.
      * Second longest range of any hero in game.
      * Can use storm and hallucinations.
      * Has regenerating shields.
  * **Weaknesses:**
      * Weakest hero in terms of health.
      * Slow acceleration and movement speed.
  * **Abilities:**
      * **White Fireball:** Creates reavers that shoot once, dealing a heavy blow to nearby enemies.
      * **Float:** Lifts the hero off the ground to escape being surrounded or become nearly invincible to lower-level enemies.
      * **Infinity Shields:** Fully regenerates hero’s shields every few seconds, extending combat time or aiding retreat.
      * **Undead Overflow:** Creates tons of zerglings that unburrow and cause havoc for nearby enemies.

### Alchemist

  * **Unit:** Firebat
  * **Health Range:** 1188–3600
  * **Damage:** 50
  * **Strengths:**
      * Can use stimpacks at any time, making him the fastest hero.
      * Has splash damage.
      * Has the second-highest amount of health.
      * Has varying discounts on all items.
  * **Weaknesses:**
      * Most abilities don’t help with gaining experience.
      * Stimpacks cost health.
      * Can splash allies with main attack and abilities.
  * **Abilities:**
      * **Human Subjects:** Creates burrowed infested terrans underneath a nearby enemy.
      * **Flaming Cross:** Creates explosions in a cross formation around the hero.
      * **Time Bomb:** Places a bomb on the hero that explodes a few seconds later in a large radius.
      * **Turkifry:** Turns most enemies around the hero into harmless and wandering turkeys.

## Abilities

All four heroes have four unique abilities. Only Level 1 abilities can be used at the start by making a Zealot at the **Gateway (Abilities)**.

  * **Unlocking:** Level up your hero by killing enemies.
      * Level 2: 1st Level Up
      * Level 3: 2nd Level Up
      * Level 4: 3rd Level Up
  * **Mastery:** Visit the **Heroes Guild** to learn new abilities via the "Level Up" menu option.
  * **Mana Costs:**
      * Level 1: 200
      * Level 2: 350
      * Level 3: 500
      * Level 4: 1000
  * **Stages:** Abilities have 3 stages of power. Each "Stage Up" costs **100 minerals**.
      * Stage 2: Reach Level 5
      * Stage 3: Reach Level 10
      * Stage 4: Reach Level 15

> **Important Notes:**
>
>   * Abilities cannot be used in buildings, except during boss battles.
>   * Level 4 abilities are disabled during boss battles.
>   * Level 1 abilities can be "chained" (used consecutively while the first effect lingers) to increase effectiveness.

## Quests

  * **Main Quests:** Follow the central story to beat the game.
  * **Side Quests:** Explore the map and earn money.
  * **Inventory:** Move your dropship (**Inventory**) to the right to view active quests.
  * **Acceptance:** Move your hero to the civilian and **Unload Slot 1**. The Inventory must be red for the acceptance to register correctly.

### Side Quest Locations

#### Slenzar (Available after town repair)

1.  **Kill the Lifted Ones:** Take down flying units in the Plains/Rainfall Way.
2.  **Stun the Vampires:** Lure Vampires into the stun beacon in the Cave.
3.  **Teleport to Slenzar:** Find the secret teleport beacon in the Plains and use it.

#### Tymmini

1.  **Gather Spider Blood:** Kill Spiders to the North and run over carcasses to collect blood.
2.  **The Mysterious Triangle:** Place units next to the three snake statues in the Valley of Truth.
3.  **Find the Lost Sons:** Escort two sons from the Goodbye Route (West) back to their father. Failure occurs if a son dies.

#### Gixon

1.  **Kill Mongrel Burrows:** Destroy eggs in the Higher Plains to unleash/kill zerglings.
2.  **Find the Missing Boats:** Push floating boats back upstream.
3.  **Kill the Shifting Ones:** Use two Dynamites to blow up boulders North of town to reach the lurkers.

#### Malaek

1.  **Defend the Base:** Keep the base outside town alive for the allotted time.
2.  **Destroy the Military Base:** Sneak to the top-left base and plant bombs on four Suppressors.
3.  **Find the Five Horses:** Locate and bring five free-roaming horses back to the master.

## Energy

Moving the hero consumes energy (top left).

  * **Depletion:** If energy hits 0, health is set to 1% and cannot be healed further.
  * **Replenishing:** Eat **Food** (Market/Science Facility) or drink **Beverages** (Bar/Factory).
  * **Stamina:** Stamina grows every 4 levels, reducing energy loss while moving.
  * **Drunkenness:** Drinks provide high energy but cause dizziness/uncontrolled movement.
  * **Revival:** On death, energy drains 5x faster. If energy hits 0 before a Soul Catcher is used, the game is lost.

### Foods & Drinks

  * Blueberry Pie, Pizza, Chicken, Lasagna, Cheddar Cheese, Bread, Rice & Beans, Hamburger, Sandwich, Jello.
  * Gin, Beer, Vodka, Rum, Wine.

## Buildings & Shops

Enter by moving next to the structure; exit via the **Exit beacon**.

  * **Inn:** Rest to restore full health for the hero and all recruits. Costs increase in later towns.
  * **Market:** Purchase food to sustain Energy.
  * **Item Shop:** Purchase combat and utility items.
  * **Blacksmith:** Upgrade weapons and armor.
  * **Castle:** Buyable (except Slenzar). Purchasing a town allows construction of new buildings (like Portals), earns you money spent by allies in shops, and grants discounts.
  * **Recruitment Center:** Hire fighters (**Crawler, Fanatic, Long-bow Specialist, Ranger**) or technicians (**Worker, Catapult**). Use the Dark Archon to mind control choices.
  * **Heroes Guild:** Learn/Stage Up abilities.
  * **Stable:** Buy vehicles (**Horse, Carriage**) for safe travel. Vehicles do zero damage. Exit horses with a mine; exit carriages via siege mode.
  * **Trader:** Sell valuables (**Diamond, Jade, Moonstone, Sapphire, Onyx, Pearl**). Harmony Gems are special.
  * **Town Portal:** Teleport between any of the four towns for free by unloading the correct slot.
